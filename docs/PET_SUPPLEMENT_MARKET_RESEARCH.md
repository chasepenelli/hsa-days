# Pet Supplement Market Research

## Market Overview

The global pet supplement market was valued at approximately $2.4 billion in 2023 and is projected to reach $4.5 billion by 2030, growing at a CAGR of ~9.5%. Key growth drivers include increasing pet humanization, rising awareness of preventive health care, and the aging pet population.

## Cancer-Specific Segment

The canine cancer supplement niche is small but growing rapidly:
- **Estimated TAM:** $150-200M annually (US)
- **Growth rate:** 12-15% CAGR, outpacing the broader pet supplement market
- **Key products:** Medicinal mushroom extracts (Turkey Tail, Reishi), fish oil, turmeric/curcumin, CBD
- **Distribution:** Primarily online DTC and specialty veterinary clinics

## Competitive Landscape

### General Pet Supplement Brands
- **Nutramax (Denamarin/Dasuquin):** Market leader in liver/joint support. Strong vet channel.
- **Zesty Paws:** DTC leader on Amazon. Broad supplement line, heavy on treats/chews format.
- **PetHonesty:** Growing DTC brand. Similar positioning to Zesty Paws.

### Cancer-Focused Products
- **I'm-Yunity (Turkey Tail PSP):** The specific product used in the Penn Vet study. Premium pricing (~$60-80/month).
- **Real Mushrooms:** High-quality mushroom extracts. Growing among informed pet owners.
- **K9 Medicinals:** Specifically targets canine cancer. Small but dedicated following.
- **ELIAS Animal Health:** Immunotherapy approach (clinical, not supplement).

### Gaps in the Market
1. **No integrated protocol brand** — owners must source 5-8 individual supplements from different brands
2. **No HSA-specific product** — hemangiosarcoma has unique needs (bleeding, anemia, cardiac)
3. **Dosing confusion** — no weight-bracketed, pre-portioned solutions
4. **Quality inconsistency** — mushroom extract potency varies wildly between brands
