## Treatment Options (MISSING FROM SITE)

### Surgery
- Splenectomy (most common): Median survival surgery alone = 1-3 months (19-86 days)
- Cardiac (right atrial): More complex, median 4 months with surgery + chemo
- Subcutaneous/dermal: Best prognosis, Stage I potentially curable (>780 days)

### Chemotherapy Protocols
| Protocol | Median Survival (Splenic) |
|---|---|
| No treatment | Days to 1-2 weeks |
| Surgery alone | 19-86 days (1-3 months) |
| Surgery + doxorubicin | 141-179 days (~5-6 months) |
| Surgery + AC protocol | 170-200 days (~6 months) |
| Surgery + doxorubicin + eBAT | 197 days (~6.5 months) |

### Immunotherapy (Emerging)
- **eBAT** (University of Minnesota): Most promising, Phase II showed 197 vs 133 days
- **ELIAS Cancer Immunotherapy**: Autologous dendritic cell vaccine, available at select centers
- **Propranolol**: Beta-blocker with anti-angiogenic properties, multiple institutions studying

## Emergency Preparedness (MISSING FROM SITE)

### Splenic Rupture Signs
- Sudden weakness/collapse, pale gums, distended abdomen, rapid/shallow breathing
- Can be fatal within 1-6 hours without intervention
- 50% of splenic masses in dogs are benign — histopathology essential

### Cardiac Tamponade Signs
- Sudden weakness, muffled heart sounds, collapse, distended jugular veins
- Treatment: pericardiocentesis (needle drainage)

### Gum Color Guide
- PINK = normal
- PALE/WHITE = anemia or blood loss — call vet urgently
- BRIGHT RED = possible sepsis
- BLUE/PURPLE = oxygen deprivation — IMMEDIATE emergency
- CRT (capillary refill): press gum, should return pink in <2 seconds

### Emergency Kit
- Yunnan Baiyao (including red emergency pill)
- Written emergency vet address/phone (24-hour ER)
- Copy of current bloodwork and medication list
- Styptic powder, gauze, non-stick pads, vet wrap

## Quality of Life Scales

### HHHHHMM Scale (Dr. Alice Villalobos)
7 parameters, each 0-10: Hurt, Hunger, Hydration, Hygiene, Happiness, Mobility, More good days than bad
- Score ≥35: acceptable QoL
- Score <35: euthanasia should be discussed
- Individual scores 0-3: serious concern regardless of total

### HSA-Specific End-of-Life Considerations
- HSA can go from "fine" to "emergency" in hours
- "Better a week too early than a day too late" principle
- Waiting for obvious decline risks emergency death vs. peaceful one
- Pre-set "non-negotiable" quality markers before crisis

## Breed Risk

### Tier 1 — Significantly elevated (2-5x)
- **Golden Retriever**: ~1 in 5 lifetime risk. Screen with abdominal ultrasound from age 6-7.
- **German Shepherd**: Higher incidence of cardiac HSA. Echo screening warranted for older dogs.
- **Labrador Retriever**: High risk, especially splenic.

### Tier 2 — Moderately elevated
- Portuguese Water Dog, Bernese Mountain Dog, Flat-Coated Retriever, Boxer, Skye Terrier, English Setter
